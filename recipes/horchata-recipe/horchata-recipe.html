Horchata Recipe


Description

Horchata (pronounced or-CHAH-tah) is a popular Mexican drink that is often described as a sweet rice milk beverage. Horchata is made out of rice, milk, vanilla, and cinnamon and is served in many Mexican restaurants.

Ingredients

  • uncooked long-grain white rice
  • 2 whole cinnamon sticks
  • water
  • evaporated milk
  • milk
  • sugar
  • vanilla extract

Steps

  1. First, pour uncooked rice, 2 cups of fresh water, and cinnamon sticks into a blender. Blend on high for one minute.
  2. For the next step, add the remaining 2 cups of water and blend again on low for another minute.
  3. Allow the horchata mixture to sit at room temperature for a minimum of three hours.
  4. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve into a pitcher. Add the evaporated milk, regular milk, sugar, and vanilla. Mix well.
  5. Refrigerate until well chilled. Serve on ice and sprinkle with cinnamon, and a touch of freshly grated nutmeg, if desired.
